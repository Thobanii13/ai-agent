## AERIAL

### Lappet-faced vulture
- **Locomotion**: 1–2 wingbeats/sec, long gliding phases, steep descending approach,
  heavy landing with forward lurch, rolling ground gait using wing stubs for balance
- **Sensory mode**: Long-distance vision, social detection of descending vultures
- **Threat response**: Dominance display — erect lappets, spread wings, direct approach
- **Interaction vocabulary**:
  1. Primary feather micro-spread — individual feathers extend/retract for lift adjustment
  2. Head-snap scan — 90° head rotation to fix distant movement
  3. Lappet erection — facial skin folds flush deep red under threat
  4. Bill-wipe — beak wiped laterally on ground after feeding or post-conflict
  5. Mantling — wings half-spread over food/space to block competitors
  6. Hissing breath — forced exhalation during confrontation, not a vocalization

### Peregrine falcon
- **Locomotion**: Level flight 60–90 km/h, stoop dive up to 380 km/h, rapid wingbeats
  in powered flight, long glides between thermals
- **Sensory mode**: Binocular vision, tracks prey trajectory in flight
- **Threat response**: Aggressive — stoops on intruders, alarm call
- **Interaction vocabulary**:
  1. Pre-stoop circle — 2–3 tight circles before committing to dive
  2. Wing-tuck — wings fold completely against body at top speed
  3. Foot-first strike — talons lead, body follows
  4. Prey bind — talons lock closed on contact
  5. Kiting — hovering into wind before diving
  6. Caching head-bob — rapid head movements scanning after landing

## SAVANNA / OPEN GROUND

### Juvenile black rhinoceros (2 years)
- **Locomotion**: Heavy bouncing trot, body compresses visibly per step, short bursts
  to 30 km/h, abrupt stops, stiff-legged alarm walk
- **Sensory mode**: Smell dominant, hearing secondary, vision poor
- **Threat response**: Security-seeking — moves toward mother, not away from threat
- **Interaction vocabulary**:
  1. Ear swivel — both ears rotate independently toward sound source
  2. Nostril flare — nostrils widen and flex processing scent (3 slow cycles)
  3. Body press — full flank pressed against mother's side
  4. Stiff-leg trot — fast trot with stiff front legs, head held low
  5. Tail raise — short tufted tail lifts when alarmed
  6. Head swing — slow wide horizontal sweep while scenting

### Cheetah (adult female, hunting)
- **Locomotion**: Stalking walk 2–3 km/h head low; sprint 0–100 km/h in 3 seconds,
  stride up to 7 metres, spine flexes visibly with each bound
- **Sensory mode**: Vision primary — tracks prey movement, judges distance
- **Threat response**: Avoidance — will not engage larger predators
- **Interaction vocabulary**:
  1. Hard stare — head lowers, eyes lock on target, ears flatten
  2. Crouch-walk — body drops to near-ground height during stalk approach
  3. Tail twitch — tip of tail flicks left-right during pre-sprint tension
  4. Sprint collapse — after failed sprint, body drops, flanks heaving
  5. Chirp contact call — high-pitched birdlike sound to cubs
  6. Scent-mark pause — stops at raised object, sniffs, urine-marks

## UNDERGROUND / BURROWING

### Naked mole-rat (worker)
- **Locomotion**: Tunneling thrust with powerful limbs, scraping movement, can run
  backward as fast as forward, body compresses to fit tight tunnels
- **Sensory mode**: Touch and vibration primary, poor vision
- **Threat response**: Worker — alarm signal, retreats toward queen chamber
- **Interaction vocabulary**:
  1. Tooth-grind excavation — incisors chipping at soil in rapid bursts
  2. Passing nudge — physical shoulder contact when passing in tunnel
  3. Alarm squeak — high-frequency burst when disturbed
  4. Substrate kick — rear legs kick excavated soil backward in chain
  5. Grooming pause — brief self-grooming of snout after digging
  6. Sniff investigation — nose pressed to tunnel wall, rapid sampling

### Common wombat (adult, solitary)
- **Locomotion**: Heavy low-frequency gait, lumbering roll, surprisingly fast sprint
  (40 km/h), powerful digging with flat forefeet
- **Sensory mode**: Scent primary, hearing moderate, poor vision
- **Threat response**: Retreat to burrow, or stand ground and rump-present
- **Interaction vocabulary**:
  1. Rump-hump — arches back exposing cartilaginous rump as defense
  2. Scent-chin rub — chin dragged along ground marking territory
  3. Snuff-snort — sharp nasal exhale when alarmed
  4. Dig-pause — stops mid-dig, lifts head, scans, resumes
  5. Reverse-wedge — backs into tight space rump-first as protection
  6. Soil-flick — rear kick to clear excavated material

## AQUATIC / SEMI-AQUATIC

### Nile crocodile (adult, ambush)
- **Locomotion**: Belly-crawl on land (slow), high-walk (faster), explosive lunge
  from water, powerful tail-driven swimming
- **Sensory mode**: Vibration (dermal pressure receptors), vision above/below waterline
- **Threat response**: Freeze then explosive attack, territorial roar
- **Interaction vocabulary**:
  1. Stillness hold — complete motionlessness for extended periods
  2. Eye-raise — eyes and nostrils rise to surface, body submerged
  3. Throat vibration — infrasound communication, water surface ripples
  4. Tail-lash — rapid lateral tail sweep as threat or balance
  5. Gape display — mouth held open wide (thermoregulation or threat)
  6. Death-roll begin — body rotation starting slowly

## ARBOREAL

### Chimpanzee (adult male, social group)
- **Locomotion**: Knuckle-walk on ground, brachiation through canopy, vertical
  climbing with hand-over-hand, standing upright briefly for displays
- **Sensory mode**: Vision primary (trichromatic), social reading of faces/posture
- **Threat response**: Charging display, piloerection, branch-drag, coalition
- **Interaction vocabulary**:
  1. Piloerection — hair stands on end making body appear larger
  2. Branch-drag — grabs and drags dead branch noisily during display
  3. Pant-hoot — long vocalization building from soft hoots to screams
  4. Lip-smack — rapid lip movement during grooming or appeasement
  5. Buttress-drum — slaps large tree roots for long-distance communication
  6. Bared-teeth grin — submissive fear grimace (not a smile)

## DESERT / ARID

### Desert scorpion (adult, nocturnal)
- **Locomotion**: Segmented stop-start movement, 8 legs in alternating pairs,
  tail curved upward at rest, rapid 180° body rotation when threatened
- **Sensory mode**: Vibration (slit sensilla on legs detect ground movement),
  pectines detect chemical traces, low-light vision
- **Threat response**: Sting raise (tail arches forward), claw display, or rapid retreat
- **Interaction vocabulary**:
  1. Vibration freeze — all movement stops, legs spread to maximize ground contact
  2. Pectine drag — comb-like organs on underside dragged across substrate
  3. Claw-extend — pedipalps extend and open fully in threat
  4. Tail-arc — metasoma arches forward over body during threat display
  5. Stridulation — claw segments rubbed together producing rasping sound
  6. Prey-pin — claws pin prey while stinger positions

## COLD / POLAR

### Emperor penguin (adult, colony)
- **Locomotion**: Upright waddle at 2–3 km/h, toboggan slide on belly (ice),
  powerful swimming up to 22 km/h, porpoising out of water
- **Sensory mode**: Vision (UV-sensitive), hearing for chick/mate recognition
- **Threat response**: Huddle tighter, redirect aggression toward edges
- **Interaction vocabulary**:
  1. Braying call — loud trumpeting call for mate/chick recognition
  2. Ecstatic display — head thrown back, flippers extended, call given
  3. Huddle shuffle — tiny coordinated steps inward against wind
  4. Flipper-slap — sharp lateral flipper strike as aggression
  5. Regurgitation bob — head pumping motion before feeding chick
  6. Pebble presentation — male offers pebble to female as courtship
